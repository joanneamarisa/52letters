34. A few of my favourite things

by Joanne Amarisa

Melbourne, Australia. Thursday, 16 March 2023.

My Dearest Friend,

Aside from you, this letter lands in the mailbox of 95 other people, which is about 94 more than what I anticipated when I first started writing letters. So thank you :) for being here and reading all this time.

On to the letter — quick and light this time! I thought I'd share with you a list of favourites (that I can remember) – some recent, some accumulated from the past several years, to hopefully add to your lists and maybe brighten up your day sometime.

There'll be a lot of links, so I hope Gmail forgives me and doesn't mark me as spam. If it doesn't, and this letter does find you safely, enjoy :)
 


Favourite movie: About Time (2013)

Favourite quote from that movie: "I'd only give one piece of advice to anyone marrying. We're all quite similar in the end. We all get old and tell the same tales too many times. But try and marry someone kind."

Favourite book series: 
  • The Thursday Murder Club by Richard Osman, and
  • The Hunger Games by Suzanne Collins
Favourite author: Jodi Picoult

Favourite poet: Sarah Kay

Favourite spoken word poem: Table Games by Sarah Kay

Favourite thing to read online: Modern Love column by the New York Times.

Favourite websites: 
  • The Pudding — Data-driven stories from the US 
  • Kontinentalist — Data-driven stories about Asia
  • The Loneliness Project — Shared stories about loneliness
  • Window Swap — Watch the view from a stranger's window
Favourite YouTube channels: 
  • Vox Media
  • Great Big Story
  • Architectural Digest — Just house goals!
  • Menjadi Manusia — A safe space for real, human perspectives
Favourite podcast: How to be a Better Human from the TED Audio Collective. I especially love it for the host, comedian Chris Duffy, who brings a very personable, lighthearted, yet intentional approach to each grand topic, and never ends an episode without giving us practical advice.

Favourite soundtrack: If Beale Street Could Talk by Nicholas Brittell.

Runner-up favourite soundtrack: Pixar Animation's Soul OST by Jon Batiste

Favourite musical artists: Yura Yunita, Tulus, Sara Bareilles, Leslie Odom Jr, Samm Henshaw, Olivia Dean, NIKI, wow I never should've started this list.... let's try something else.

Favourite painter: Vilhelm Hammershøi. He is a Danish painter known for painting windows, capturing sunlight in subdued tones and mundane, everyday scenes. I find it very peaceful!


Favourite Tiny Desk Concert: Coldplay's on NPR, and HONNE's on Folkative

Favourite musical: West Side Story, and the Sound of Music. Although I need to watch more!

Favourite dance performances: Keone and Mari's first show in World of Dance 2017, Brotherhood Dance Crew at Feel the Bounce 2017, and a funny ballet show by Vienna State Opera

Favourite live performance: Olivia Dean live at the Jazz Cafe, London.

Three favourite places to eat in Melbourne:
  • Isan Soul
  • Fonda Mexican
  • Etto Pasta Bar
Places that have warm, cozy ambience — great for enjoying comfort, can-never-go-wrong food with your people :)

Favourite food from those places:
  • Isan Soul: The soft shell crab is the best-seller!
  • Fonda Mexican: It's Mexican! So anything goes
  • Etto Pasta Bar: The garlic bread
Favourite K-Drama: Extraordinary Attorney Woo Young Woo is probably the freshest one I've seen recently and its variety of cases makes it more memorable. It's also just charming, sweet, and feel-good.

Favourite walking trail in Melbourne: Go from Flinders Street Station, all the way to Shrine of Remembrance, and then into the Tan Tracks, and towards north back out to the Yarra river.

Favourite park: Fawkner Park

Favourite cafes: I love cafes that help you hide. So Tori's and Little Rogue are probably up there on my list, when it's not inundated with tourists. But! I also love cafes for the kind owners I come across, and there are two that were part of my commute back when I used to live in the suburbs.

The first is Leaf Lane, located right across the street from Hawksburn Station — the owner Tony is very polite and sincere. Stopping by his cafe is probably the thing I miss the most since moving.

The other is, if you stop at Armadale Station, take the neighbourhood exit (not King Arcade) and cross the intersection to Coin Laundry Cafe. Joy, a petite, sprightly young woman who runs the cafe with her family, lives up to her name. She will bring the sweetest, brightest smile to start your day, with her very cheerful and welcoming greetings of "Oh, how are you! It's good to see you!"

Favourite view in Melbourne: Anything around the Yarra River.

Favourite building in Melbourne: The Regent Theatre is pretty hard to beat. Just takes my breath away every time.

Favourite book store: Readings, and Coventry Bookstore.

Favourite thing about Melbourne: My friends :)

Favourite thing to do when I get home: Not do anything. Most days I'll still have personal emails to reply or tasks to finish. But at least once a week I try to reserve an empty evening, where I can put on some music and spend an hour in the kitchen cooking, or in my living room reading, uninterrupted, and be accompanied only by nice tunes and warm mood light.

Favourite thing to cook: I'll be basic and say pasta. But I've also been dying to remake this donabe recipe.


Favourite place to find recipes: What to Cook Today, Recipe Tin Eats, and Happy Donabe Life for Asian staples. For desserts, I actually love Clare Saffitz and Samantha Seneviratne (on YouTube.)

Favourite music to put on while cooking: Joseph Solomon's all about love playlist.

Favourite TV shows: Abbott Elementary and Modern Family (if you like warm and fuzzy), or Castle and Criminal Minds (if you like psychopaths).

Favourite dance floor song: September by Earth Wind & Fire

Another favourite dance floor song: Save the Last Dance for Me by Michael Bublé

Another another favourite dance floor song: More by Bobby Darin

Favourite song for when it's gloomy or raining: The Very Thought of You by Jon Batiste

Favourite TED Talks: There are so many, but three that's still stuck to me is How to do laundry when you're depressed by KC Davis, Who are you, really? by Brian Little, and Quit social media by Cal Newport.

Favourite mode of writing: Journaling! But letters are a very very close second ;)
 

What are some of your favourite things?

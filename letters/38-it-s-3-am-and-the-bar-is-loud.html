38. It's 3 AM, and the bar is loud

by Joanne Amarisa
Melbourne, Australia. Friday, 21 April 2023.

My Dear Friend,

I'm writing this to you from a hidden gem in the city that's no longer hidden. If you ever find yourself in the Melbourne CBD craving for a great cup of matcha (or hojicha), Tori's on Niagara Ln is where you want to go. (Not sponsored.) I must say, though, arriving at lunch time is normally the best time. And by that I mean, the most possible time to find comfortable seating that won't force you to sit shoulder-to-shoulder with 2 strangers in a bench made for two and a half people. Anyway. That's where I am! Having arrived at 12pm, I'm comfortably nested in a brown armchair that I absolutely have overstayed my welcome in.

Earlier this week, I was on a phone call with one of my past university lecturers. She mentors me for my passion project-slash-side hustle that I do outside of my work. I was catching her up about my experience running an in-person class last month for the first time ever. (Oh, this part is most definitely definitely endorsed.)

On top of that, I was asking her for advice on an application I'm writing, in which I have to explain about the project in great detail. For something I've been juggling for the past one and a half years, I find it hard nowadays to actually explain what the project is about. It's what happens when we're so deep in the weeds of things. It's like when you have spent such a long time knowing a person, that when someone asks you for the first time, What are they like? You are at a loss for words, thinking, Where do I begin?

So anyway, here is what my mentor said (paraphrased by memory) :

I think you can be generous about this project's description. When you speak about the details, don't assume the person on the other end understands what it is you do as a whole. And if they're reading and thinking, What the heck is she talking about? They're not going to continue, they're just gonna put it away.

I think you need to spell it out. How would you explain it to my daughter, who is 10 years old? How would you explain it to my daughter's teacher?

If you had to yell it across a bar and it's 3 in the morning and you need to grab their attention, what would that be?

That last metaphor has stuck to me since, soon becoming my favourite way of thinking about how to explain anything: this project, or my life as a whole.

I'd like to explore what it means to live in more brazen honesty. Who am I, what are my dreams, according to that version of myself yelling across the bar at 3 AM?

(Regardless of how she got there in the first place... I mean it depends, what bar is it? Is the music great? I hope she gets home safely.)

We're blanketed with facades. We've conjured up so many fronts about our truest desires, our most unguarded selves, that when asked to strip it down to just that one loud, abrupt, simple sentence, we have a hard time finding the words.

But it's night time. And the bar is loud. And we're all here because we all feel a little lost (or perhaps, this is where we feel the most found.)

When the stakes are none, and the rest of the world is too asleep to be listening, what do we say of us? What would we like to become of us? Drunk or sober, would we recognise those words? 

Are we living a life worth shouting for, to us?
 

This week I'm reading:
Mere Christianity by C. S. Lewis

This week I'm listening to:
You'll Be in My Heart - Spotify Singles cover by NIKI

36. One and one hundred: Write your own letter

by Joanne Amarisa
Melbourne, Australia. Thursday, 6 April 2023.

"Even if I knew that tomorrow the world would go to pieces, I would still plant my apple tree."
– Martin Luther

My Dear Friend,

I read the quote above in the most recent book I finished. (Review here.) What I love about the quote, I think, is its sweet balance between relenting to what we can't control, and doing what we can.

Without me realising, we have recently reached the one-year anniversary of 52 Letters from Jo. The first ever letter I wrote was sent on 26th March 2022. How the year has flown!

We happened to reach 100 readers throughout this time. So in this letter, I personally wanted to thank you for being here, for reading, for every kind message or reply you've sent in response, and simply for giving my words, which are far from perfect, a special corner of your inbox every week.

I promised myself I wouldn't go into a long spiel into why the letters exist. I do wish that through reading each letter, you may understand why they exist.

But to put it in short: We all need our apple trees. The world is crumbling, the honeybees fear for their lives, and nobody's talking to each other anymore. But even if things vanish tomorrow, if nothing goes my way, if our earth finally gives way and there's nothing left for us to take for granted, I still want to plant my apple tree.

I still want to sow where I can, water as long as my feet allow, wait for signs of sapling with every last bit of hope.

And in a very metaphorical way, I hope that explains why I write letters.

As I'm writing this to you right now, I'm about an hour away from meeting a friend to visit an art exhibition. (So I must start getting ready.) But! I didn't want to miss this chance to thank you, and to celebrate our one year and one hundred readers.

On this 36th letter, I've opened up a space for you to write a letter too!

It doesn't have to be a letter to me — it can be to yourself, or to anybody. Some creative prompts: Share a story from your life that makes you smile; write a postcard from wherever in the world you are now; write a letter to your past or future self; raise burning questions about writing, life, or love; ask for advice on something that's been on your mind.

Each response will be anonymous, and handled with great care.

You can submit your letter here. In the same link, you can also tell me what you'd like to see more of from 52 Letters from Jo.

I can't wait to read what you write. I'm hoping you do! But if not, I'm grateful you're here either way.

Thank you again. See you in the next one.

Best wishes,

Jo

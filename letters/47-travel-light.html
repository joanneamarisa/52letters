47. Travel light

by Joanne Amarisa
Wednesday, 25 October 2023. Melbourne, Australia.

My Dearest Friend,

Do you know how a rocket ship gets launched into outer space? Okay, maybe the answer to this is literal rocket science... but I was watching a video yesterday by Ezinne Zara, and she mentioned what has to happen in order for a rocket to ascend.

But before I tell you, I might redirect us to something far older than space travel. It is a story in the Bible about a woman named Hannah.

The Bible tells us that though Hannah was a wife, she was barren and couldn't conceive a child. It's difficult to deny as you read her chapter, just how much this grieved her deeply. She would cry, lose her appetite, go into temples and cry some more, inconsolably.

Until one day, Hannah went into a sanctuary, and finally uttered a brokenhearted prayer.

She said, "God, if you will take a look at my pain, answer my prayer and give me a son, then I’ll give him back completely—unreservedly—to you."

The final part of her words is what I couldn't comprehend: If you give me what I want, I'll give it back to you. What do you mean you're giving back something you were asking for? Something you even rightfully deserve?

And here, I shall bring us back to space travel. The way a rocket ship flies into space is that the more weight it can shed away, the higher it can go into the atmosphere. The rocket needs to carry or hold onto less things, in order to ascend.

Can it be that the answer to our biggest questions is not to want more things, but to loosen our grip?

For many of us, we are under the guise that the older you become, the more things you have to carry or hold onto. This is true materially—you now have a career, a job, a home, car and health insurance, mortgage, savings, maybe a family or a child. Our grip tightens around our treasures, and we guard it with everything we've got.

Where we may sometimes be misguided is by thinking we need to have all these Things in order for life to be fulfilling.

It can even be arbitrary things. For a long time in my life, even before adulthood, that Thing for me was knowing. Every time someone asked me what I'll be doing with my life, I always had an answer—and when I realised that my answers were answers that would leave people nodding, proud, it was enough to comfort me, regardless of whether or not I was certain of it myself.

I sought assurance in knowing what my next steps were going to look like at any given time—and I'd always find it, becoming better and better at convincing myself that I'm smart enough to always know what I want to do 5-10 years down the line.

Disclaimer: I do not. Of course I do not. In fact one day, I was forced to reach a moment where I had no idea where to go from here. Plans fail and twist, as you can imagine.

I don't know what this circumstance "meant", I don't know what my next step from here is going to look like, I don't know if any of my past played a role in how things turned out. For many weeks, I, too, found myself losing appetite, hiding away in my own sanctuaries, crying inconsolably. 

But recently, Dear Friend, amidst my strickenness and confusion, I found a sliver of peace. Not peace from finding an answer, because I hadn't. Not peace from mind-mapping, forecasting, figuring out my next move. Not peace from getting more things. Not even peace from a divine Aha! moment of revelation, because I still felt (*feel) just as lost.

Pacing around in my bedroom, I simply thought, "Maybe this is the season where I don't know."

And I properly breathed for the first time in weeks. My treasure was approval, and certainty. And that afternoon, it finally fell away.

I am learning what it means to shed that weight. I am learning what it means to loosen our grip, in order to move forward.

"I must travel light in this world," Ezinne says. Going back to Hannah, she desired for a child so much that it overtook her with grief, until she said, If I were given a child tomorrow, I'd hand it back to you. She finally released.

Traveling light may look different for different people. It might be sacrificing some things in order to make more space for you; it might be letting go of the need to please people's opinions; it might be hitting pause on your endless pursuit of The Thing and simply giving into the rest provided in the present season, regardless of where it takes you.

It can look like many different things, because that is faith. It is assurance in the things we hope for, though we don't see it. Though we can't have it in the palm of our hands right now. But that means we must learn to travel light.

So that we can continue onward. Because the joy promised in the journey is real and there for us, too. So much so, that we may even look up towards that atmosphere, and be so in awe of what awaits us, that we pay no attention to the things that needed to fall away.
 

What does "traveling light" look like for you?
